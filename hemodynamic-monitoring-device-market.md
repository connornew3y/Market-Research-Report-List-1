<p><p>The hemodynamic monitoring device market is categorized into three main types: invasive, minimally invasive, and non-invasive. Invasive devices require catheter insertion into blood vessels, providing precise and continuous pressure measurements, often used in critical care settings. Minimally invasive devices utilize smaller access points for monitoring, reducing complications while still offering reliable data. Non-invasive devices measure hemodynamic parameters without penetration, using techniques like ultrasound or electrical impedance, making them suitable for routine checks and broader patient populations.</p></p>
