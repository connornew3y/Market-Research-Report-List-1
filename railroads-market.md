<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Railroads operate in two primary market types: Type I and Type II. </p><p>Type I markets are characterized by a single service provider monopolizing the infrastructure, such as a dedicated railway line, with limited competition. This allows for pricing power and stable revenues. </p><p>Type II markets feature multiple service providers sharing the infrastructure, fostering competition and potentially lowering prices for consumers. Here, various operators may access the same tracks, encouraging innovation and diverse service offerings within the railway system.</p></p>
